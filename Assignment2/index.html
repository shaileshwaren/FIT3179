Population

The term "population" simply denotes the total number of people residing in a given area or region. It acts as a fundamental measure for comprehending how society, economies and environments dynamically change. Analysing population data offers insights into demographics, growth patterns, and numerous factors that affect the prosperity and development of a specific region.

We have three types of population to examine here; population, urban population and labour force participation population Urban population being the number of people residing in cities and urban areas. This aids leaders in addressing difficulties specific to urban contexts in the areas of infrastructure, housing, transportation, and social services. Labour force here denotes the population that are in the workforce or job market. This gives a rough picture of employment rates, skills availability and economic development opportunities.

Life Expectancy

A person's predicted lifespan is expressed statistically as their life expectancy, which takes into account lifestyle choices, medical breakthroughs, and other pertinent aspects. It is an important gauge of a population's general health and happiness. To evaluate the standard of living and efficiency of the healthcare systems in a particular region or nation, life expectancy is frequently employed in demographic and public health studies.

We have two visualizations depicting the range of life expectancy from the year 2000 to year 2015 and the different types of mortalities. The types of mortalities displayed comprises of adult mortality, infant deaths and under five deaths shown in different regions.

This ranged dot plot shows that Africa having the lowest life expectancy and North America having the highest life expectancy in 2000. Asia with the highest life expectancy in 2015 shows that Asia is developing at a high rate. Overall, all regions have increased their maximum life expectancy.

This stacked bar chart shows three different mortality types per 1000. Africa is shown as the highest mortality rates every year in all categories. Positively, all regions show a decrease in expected mortality over the years. We are able to take a look at each year to see the mortality rate of that particular year.

GDP per Capita

A key economic measure that sheds light on the general economic health of a nation's population is the gross domestic product (GDP) per capita. It is defined as the average economic production (GDP) divided by the population of a country. GDP per capita, or gross domestic product per capita, assesses a nation's economic performance on a per-person basis.

We have two visualization depicting the GDP per capita of each country and GDP per capita of each region in the year 2015. The top few countries with the highest GDP per capita are annotated by it's country's name. We can see that European Union has the highest GDP per capita while South America having the lowest GDP per capita.

Education

Key statistics that shed light on a country's educational system and labour market include Gross Tertiary Enrollment, Gross Primary Enrollment, and Unemployment Rate (%). Awareness a population's level of educational attainment and job situation requires an awareness of these measurements.

We have two visualization depicting the development of each country which are sorted by region, gross tertiary education enrollment against gross primary education and the unemployment rate in year 2015. Gross tertiary and primary education enrollment refer to the total number of students enrolled in its respective institutions. We investigate these factors to see the relationship between them.

Developed countries refer to nations that have achieved a high level of economic prosperity. We can see here that the majority of countries in European Union are developed that includes countries like Spain, Netherlands and France. For Asia and Middle East regions, the countries with developed economies are Japan and Israel respectively.

We can see that Greece has the highest gross tertiary and primary education enrollment. On the flip side however, it also has the highest unemployment rate. We can use the sliders to filter out countries to their desired values.
